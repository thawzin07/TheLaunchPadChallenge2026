# Public Event Notes From Luma

## AI Quick Summary

The Luma page presents The LaunchPad Challenge as a student AI sprint in Singapore: build the project or research idea you actually care about, claim free API credits, submit by the deadline, and the best teams present at the AI Symposium at NUS. The exact venue address is gated behind registration.

## Public Description

The event encourages students to finally build the project they have been meaning to build. It has no fixed theme and no set problem. Participants are judged on understanding the problem and whether the solution works, not on visual polish.

The public Luma text describes the challenge as:

- A four-week open sprint for students.
- Open to any project or research idea the participant cares about.
- Supported by free API credits.
- Connected to a live presentation opportunity at the AI Symposium at NUS.
- A way to be seen by hiring managers and tech leads.

## Eligibility From Luma

- Current students in Singapore can join.
- Participants may enter solo or in a team of up to 3.
- Entry is free.
- Participants keep full ownership of what they build.
- Every team member must register separately.

## What Participants Get

- Free API credits for agent and browser tools; exact participant-credit amount was listed as not yet announced on Luma.
- Stage opportunity at NUS for top teams.
- Credit prizes.
- Exposure to hiring managers and tech leads.

## Access Limitations

- Exact address is not publicly visible.
- Registration is subject to host approval.
- Luma may ask participants to verify token ownership with a wallet during registration.
- Credit-claim forms for OpenAI and GMI Cloud are sent after registration, so the form contents are not publicly visible.
